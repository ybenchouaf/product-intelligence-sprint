# Competitive positioning — Bridgit

Source: Session 01 (2026-06-29). Facts, inferences, and speculation separated.

## Practical competitor: Excel + meetings

**FACT:** Capterra and G2 reviewers describe Excel, spreadsheets, and siloed systems as prior-state workflows (E020, E021).

**INFERENCE:** The day-to-day competitor is often **Excel plus recurring staffing meetings** — not a single point solution. **Confidence: High.**

This aligns with v0.1 seed: "The biggest competitor may still be Excel plus meetings."

## Long-run risk: suite absorption

**INFERENCE (H010):** Procore, ERP vendors, and broad construction suites may not need to beat Bridgit feature-for-feature if they offer good-enough workforce planning inside systems customers already use. **Confidence: Medium.**

**FACT (E016):** Procore Resource Management positions around labor, equipment, financials, forecasting, field communication.

**SPECULATION:** Suite "good enough" workforce modules could compress Bridgit's expansion room — **not verified** in customer win/loss data.

## Procore: partner and overlap

**FACT:** Bridgit lists Procore among integrations (v0.1 seed, E008).

**INFERENCE:** Procore is likely **both integration partner and potential strategic overlap** in accounts that use Procore Resource Management or expand RM capabilities. **Confidence: Medium-low** — plausible from public positioning; **not verified** in partnership terms or account overlap.

**Open:** Are Procore and Bridgit mostly partners today, competitors today, or future competitors? See backlog.

## ERP and HRIS boundaries

**FACT (v0.1 seed):** HRIS owns employee data; CRM owns pursuit pipeline; neither owns dynamic project workforce planning. **Confidence: High** for category boundary.

**INFERENCE:** ERP/job-cost systems (CMiC, Oracle, Trimble) are system-of-record competitors for budget context, not necessarily for staffing UX. **Confidence: Medium.**

## Craft / self-perform positioning tension

**FACT (E013):** Bridgit press emphasizes specialty contractor labor forecasting and crew management.

**FACT (E024):** Reviewer says craft expansion needs functionality and pricing-model changes.

**INFERENCE:** Competitive story for craft/self-perform may require **different product and packaging** than ENR GC workforce planning — not only GTM messaging. **Confidence: Medium.**

See H003a (GTM intent) vs H003b (product readiness).

## Assignar and field-ops overlap

**FACT (E017):** Assignar positions on crew/equipment scheduling, field ops, job costing.

**INFERENCE:** Stronger overlap in specialty/field workflows than in ENR executive planning. **Confidence: Medium** (v0.1 seed).
