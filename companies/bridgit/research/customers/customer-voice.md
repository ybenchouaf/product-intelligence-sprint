# Customer voice — Bridgit

Source: Session 01 (2026-06-29). Capterra, G2, TechCrunch. Facts labeled; inferences marked.

## Excel and spreadsheet replacement

**FACT (E020):** Capterra reviewers describe switching from Excel or replacing spreadsheet-driven resource planning.

Source: [Capterra Bridgit Bench reviews](https://www.capterra.com/p/189379/Bridgit-Bench/reviews/) — retrieved 2026-06-29. **Confidence: High.**

**FACT (E021):** G2 reviewers describe siloed systems and spreadsheets as prior-state workflow problems.

Source: [G2 Bridgit Bench reviews](https://www.g2.com/products/bridgit-bench/reviews) — retrieved 2026-06-29. **Confidence: High.**

**INFERENCE:** Excel and homegrown spreadsheets remain the core substitute Bridgit displaces — not only other SaaS. **Confidence: High** (supported by E020, E021, E010, E011).

## Reporting friction

**FACT (E022):** Capterra and G2 reviewers ask for stronger dashboards, reporting, printable outputs, and better reporting features.

Sources: Capterra and G2 (links above) — retrieved 2026-06-29. **Confidence: High.**

**INFERENCE:** Users still export or manually prepare narratives for executives — reporting artifacts may matter as much as planning UI. **Confidence: Medium-high.** Supports H009.

## Skills and experience model limits

**FACT (E023):** G2 reviewer says skill tracking supports simple tagging but lacks deeper skill level / experience modeling.

Source: G2 — retrieved 2026-06-29. **Confidence: Medium-high.**

**INFERENCE:** Internal Resumes / experience tracking may be strategically important but product-depth may lag messaging. **Confidence: Medium.** Qualifies H005.

## IT, CIO, and business systems in the buying orbit

**FACT (E026):** Capterra reviewers include CIO, VP Technology Services, Applications Administrator, Business Application Manager, COO, HR, and workforce roles.

Source: Capterra — retrieved 2026-06-29. **Confidence: Medium-high.**

**INFERENCE:** IT and business-systems roles appear in adoption and buying orbit — not only operations and HR. Whether CIO is buyer, sponsor, or blocker is **unknown**. **Confidence: Medium-high** for presence; **low** for role classification.

## Craft expansion friction

**FACT (E024):** Capterra reviewer says craft-resource expansion would require functionality and pricing-model adjustment.

Source: Capterra — retrieved 2026-06-29. **Confidence: Medium-high.**

**INFERENCE:** GTM intent toward craft/self-perform (E013, seed) may run ahead of product and packaging readiness. **Confidence: Medium.** Split per H003a / H003b.

## Workforce intelligence framing (context)

**FACT (E018):** TechCrunch described Bridgit as a construction "workforce intelligence" company in Oct 2021 — before current AI-first homepage framing.

Source: [TechCrunch Oct 2021](https://techcrunch.com/2021/10/26/bridgit/) — retrieved 2026-06-29. **Confidence: High.**

Source: [ITQlick pricing](https://www.itqlick.com/bridgit-bench/pricing) — retrieved 2026-06-29. **Confidence: Low** — third-party, AI-assisted, not Bridgit-confirmed.
