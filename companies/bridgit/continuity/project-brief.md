# Project brief — Bridgit

## Sprint intent

Understand Bridgit deeply enough to inform product discovery: how the company positions itself, who it serves, how the product works, where it is headed, where workflow friction exists, and what prototype opportunities are grounded in evidence.

## Current positioning (v0.1)

**FACT:** Bridgit positions as AI workforce planning software for construction contractors — not broad construction project management. Public product story: project planning, internal resumes, forecasting, Bridgit AI, integrations, communication.

**FACT:** Direction stated in seed: contractor workforce data system of record + forecasting + AI-assisted planning + enterprise integrations.

**INFERENCE:** Strategic wedge is workforce intelligence between CRM/pursuit, HRIS/ERP, PM systems, and executive decision-making — not scheduling alone. Confidence: Medium (interpretive; supported by integration narrative).

## AI strategy (public)

**FACT:** Ask Bridgit, Import Roles, Smart Suggestions / Smart Suggestion Summaries. Emphasis on messy-data intake, natural-language querying, explainable recommendations, construction-specific planning.

## Prototype direction (inference from seed)

**INFERENCE:** Highest-value prototype space is AI-supported planning artifacts — staffing meeting briefs, scenario explainers, hiring handoff packets, data quality checks, executive workforce health reports — not generic AI chat. Confidence: Medium.

Seed recommends **Staffing Meeting Brief** or **Scenario Explainer** as first prototype lane.

## Research boundaries (v0.1)

This pass excluded: customer calls, internal docs, pricing, sales process, archived job postings, direct product access.
